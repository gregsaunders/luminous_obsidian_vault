## Phase 1: Context & Scope

1. **Kickoff & Purpose**
    - **Objective**: Align stakeholders on why Business Capabilities matter—demonstrating how they define the **“what”** of the organization (rather than “how” or “who”).
    - **Activities**:
        - Educate the client on Ardoq’s capability modeling benefits (e.g., improved alignment between strategy and execution, clearer change impacts).
        - Gather initial objectives (e.g., strategic planning, system rationalization, M&A readiness).
        
2. **Define Engagement Scope**
    - **Objective**: Decide which domains or lines of business to include in this first iteration.
    - **Activities**:
        - Clarify level of detail: Will you go deep on one department or take a broad, high-level approach across multiple units?
        - Set timeframe, resources, and success metrics (e.g., number of capabilities identified, clarity on critical ones).

**Value** Ensures everyone knows why they’re identifying capabilities and what success looks like, preventing scope creep.

---

## Phase 2: Data Gathering & Preliminary Capability Identification

1. **Review Existing Documentation**
    - **Objective**: Collect any org charts, process maps, strategic goals, product listings, or prior capability definitions.
    - **Activities**:
        - Conduct a short document audit to gather relevant material, highlighting recurring themes.
        - Interview select SMEs (Subject Matter Experts) to grasp the big-picture functions.
    
2. **Initial Capability List (Draft)**
    - **Objective**: Start forming a draft list of business capabilities.
    - **Activities**:
        - Categorize potential capabilities into functional groupings (e.g., “Sales Management,” “Product Development,” “Supply Chain Coordination”).
        - Note any major overlaps or gaps that might need deeper discussion.

**Value**: Establishes a foundation to refine in collaborative sessions, preventing “blank page” syndrome during workshops.

---

## Phase 3: Collaborative Workshops

1. **Cross-Functional Capability Workshops**
    - **Objective**: Finalize and validate the organization’s capabilities with diverse stakeholders.
    - **Activities**:
        - Present the draft capability list, solicit feedback, and merge duplicates or refine definitions.
        - Use sticky notes or digital boards for interactive sessions, ensuring each function is heard.
    - **Outcome**: A consensus-driven capability hierarchy (top-level “Domains” with sub-capabilities).

2. **Criticality & Maturity Assessment**    
    - **Objective**: Assign importance and current maturity levels to each capability (e.g., High, Medium, Low).
    - **Activities**:
        - Discuss business impact of each capability on strategy, revenue, or compliance.
        - Rate maturity (e.g., “Emerging” vs. “Optimized”), capturing _current_ effectiveness.
    - **Outcome**: A prioritized list, highlighting where to invest or improve first.

**Value**: Delivers a shared mental model of the organization’s capabilities, aligned with strategic priorities.

---

## Phase 4: Modeling in Ardoq

1. **Create/Configure Metamodel Components**    
    - **Objective**: Set up Ardoq with a Business Capability component type, plus related types (e.g., Processes, Applications, Data Objects).
    - **Activities**:
        - Define naming conventions, attributes (capability owner, maturity rating, strategic importance).
        - Ensure relationships (e.g., “Capability X uses Application Y,” “Capability Z supports Strategic Objective A”).
    
2. **Import or Manually Input Capabilities**    
    - **Objective**: Populate Ardoq with the validated capability structure from the workshops.
    - **Activities**:
        - Either import from spreadsheets or documents, or manually create each capability in Ardoq.
        - Assign ownership (e.g., departmental or executive sponsor) to each capability.
    - **Outcome**: A visual, dynamic representation of the organization’s Business Capabilities.

3. **Map Dependencies & Relationships**
    - **Objective**: Show how capabilities interrelate with processes, data, technology, or other capabilities.
    - **Activities**:
        - Link each capability to the process flows (BPMN) or decision logic (DMN), if relevant.
        - Illustrate cross-capability dependencies (e.g., “Capability A depends on Capability B to function effectively”).
    - **Outcome**: An interlinked Ardoq model revealing gaps, overlaps, or potential constraints.

**Value**: Gives real-time impact analysis, enabling what-if scenarios (e.g., “What happens if we change or discontinue this capability?”).

---

## Phase 5: Validate & Analyze

1. **Internal Review**    
    - **Objective**: Present the capability model back to key stakeholders for feedback and final consensus.
    - **Activities**:
        - Walk through key relationships and any discrepancies (e.g., capabilities missing owners, unclear boundaries).
        - Adjust the Ardoq model to fix errors or refine definitions.

2. **Identify Gaps & Redundancies**    
    - **Objective**: Surface white spaces (capabilities that should exist but don’t) or redundant capabilities (overlap).
    - **Activities**:
        - Highlight opportunities for cost savings (e.g., redundant applications supporting the same capability).
        - Pinpoint capabilities that are critical but under-resourced or misaligned.
    - **Outcome**: A priority list for future improvements or consolidations.

**Value**: Generates actionable insights about where the organization can optimize or invest—ensuring quick wins (e.g., merging duplicate efforts) and longer-term transformation.

---

## Phase 6: Roadmap & Next Steps

1. **Future-State Capability Vision**    
    - **Objective**: Align capabilities to strategic goals for the medium to long term.
    - **Activities**:
        - Discuss how each capability needs to evolve (e.g., “modernize data analytics,” “automate manual workflows”).
        - Map these improvements in Ardoq, showing desired future maturity levels.

2. **Action Plan / Quick Wins**    
    - **Objective**: Identify **immediate opportunities** (low-hanging fruit) that can deliver **visible improvements**rapidly.
    - **Activities**:
        - Use capability analysis to find areas with **high impact** but **low complexity** (e.g., reassign staff, implement basic tool support).
        - Outline a short timeline with **responsibilities** and **KPIs**.

1. **Long-Term Transformation**    
    - **Objective**: Plan for larger, systemic changes involving multi-year roadmaps or deep process redesign.
    - **Activities**:
        - Integrate with other frameworks (e.g., ToC for constraint identification, BPMN for process clarity, DMN for decision logic).
        - Align major initiatives (like ERP replacements, new product lines, or org structure overhauls) with capability improvements.

**Value**: Ensures the engagement does more than create a static capability map—clients have a practical, phased plan for continuous improvement and quick ROI.

---

## Phase 7: Governance & Continuous Maintenance

1. **Model Stewardship**    
    - **Objective**: Designate model owners who keep the Ardoq repository up to date.
    - **Activities**:
        - Establish version control, guidelines for adding or modifying capabilities, and review cycles (quarterly, annually).
        - Train a small internal team to manage ongoing updates.

1. **Periodic Review**    
    - **Objective**: Ensure capabilities remain current as business needs change.
    - **Activities**:
        - Reassess capability definitions, dependencies, and maturity levels at strategic checkpoints.
        - Integrate new processes, acquisitions, or major product changes.

**Value**: Maintains a living, trusted single source of truth for organizational planning, driving faster, informed decisionsabout resource allocation, partnerships, or expansions.

---

## Why This Framework Delivers Quick Wins & Value

1. **Early Stakeholder Alignment**
    - Engaging cross-functional teams from the outset fosters buy-in and reduces duplication, enabling swift action on high-ROI improvements.

2. **Holistic Organizational View**    
    - Ardoq’s visual model clarifies how each capability interacts with technology, data, and processes—minimizing guesswork in change initiatives.

3. **Action-Oriented Insights**    
    - Identifying gaps, redundancies, and priority areas paves the way for rapid fixes (quick wins) and longer-term strategic plans.

4. **Scalable & Sustainable**    
    - By establishing governance and model ownership, organizations can easily adapt the capability map as they grow, ensuring ongoing alignment with evolving goals.

5. **Bridge to Other Frameworks**    
    - The Ardoq Metamodel for capabilities can plug in seamlessly with Story Mapping, BPMN/DMN, or ToC to provide deeper insights into root causes, constraints, and processes.

## Conclusion
This engagement framework walks clients through a step-by-step process for defining and mapping Business Capabilities in Ardoq. By combining collaborative workshops, practical action steps, and ongoing governance, organizations gain a clear, dynamic overview of what they do—enabling faster decisions, targeted improvements, and cost-effective transformation in sync with broader business objectives.
