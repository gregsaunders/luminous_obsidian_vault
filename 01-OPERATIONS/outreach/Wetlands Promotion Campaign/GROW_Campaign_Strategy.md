# GROW Project Social Media Campaign Strategy
**Campaign Period:** November 6, 2025 - April 2, 2026
**Primary Platform:** LinkedIn + Targeted Email Outreach
**Posting Frequency:** Weekly posts (approximately 22 weeks)

---

## Campaign Overview

### Objectives
1. **Raise awareness** of the University of Calgary GROW project's breakthrough research in constructed treatment wetlands for oil sands tailings remediation
2. **Position you as a thought leader** providing business perspective on environmental innovation in the oil sands sector
3. **Promote Luminous Bio Solutions'** role in optimizing wetlands through bacterial biosensor technology
4. **Engage key stakeholders** including oil sands operators, regulators, academics, and environmental consultants
5. **Build momentum** around the viable, cost-effective solution to Alberta's 1.4 trillion litre tailings challenge

### Target Audiences
- **Primary:** Oil sands operators & executives (Suncor, Syncrude, CNRL, Imperial Oil, Cenovus)
- **Secondary:** Environmental regulators & policymakers (AER, Environment Canada, Alberta government)
- **Tertiary:** Academic community, environmental consultants, and engineering firms

### Campaign Tone
**Optimistic Thought Leader** - Enthusiastic about scientific breakthroughs while acknowledging the scale of the challenge. Forward-thinking industry voice that bridges science and business.

## LinkedIn Post Format Template

### Structure
1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) - Grab attention with surprising fact or provocative question
2. **Context** (2-3 sentences) - Situate the topic within the larger challenge
3. **Key Insight** (3-4 sentences) - Main message, research finding, or business perspective
4. **Luminous Connection** (1-2 sentences) - How your company contributes to the solution
5. **Engagement Question** (1 sentence) - Invite comments and discussion
6. **Hashtags** (5-8 relevant tags)

### Length Guidelines
- **Optimal:** 150-250 words
- **Maximum:** 300 words (LinkedIn truncates at ~210 characters in feed)

### Visual Content
- **Featured researchers** from seminar posters
- **Infographics** on tailings scale, wetland processes, biosensor methodology
- **Data visualizations** from presentations (with attribution)
- **Curl wetland photos** if available
- **Video content** generated via AI tools

## Email Outreach Strategy

### Email Series Structure

**Email 1: Introduction** (Send Week 1)
- Subject: "Introducing a Business Perspective on GROW Research - Oil Sands Tailings Solutions"
- Purpose: Introduce yourself, Luminous Bio Solutions, and your intent to share insights
- Call to Action: Watch for updates as seminar series progresses

**Email 2-4: Quarterly Updates** (Weeks 6, 12, 18)
- Subject variations focused on specific breakthroughs or themes
- Purpose: Provide curated synthesis of presentations relevant to recipient's role
- Call to Action: Engage in conversation, share perspectives, explore collaboration

**Email 5: Series Wrap-Up** (Week 22)
- Subject: "GROW Seminar Series Conclusion - What's Next for Tailings Remediation?"
- Purpose: Summarize key findings, present business case, propose next steps
- Call to Action: Schedule meeting to discuss application to their operations

### Email Segmentation

**Segment A: Oil Sands Operators**
- Focus: Operational feasibility, cost-effectiveness, regulatory pathway
- Key contacts: Environmental managers, tailings leads, sustainability directors

**Segment B: Regulators & Policymakers**
- Focus: Monitoring capabilities, compliance frameworks, environmental outcomes
- Key contacts: AER staff, Alberta Environment officials, federal regulators

**Segment C: Academic & Research Community**
- Focus: Scientific methodology, interdisciplinary collaboration, future research
- Key contacts: University researchers, graduate students, research institutes

**Segment D: Consultants & Engineers**
- Focus: Implementation strategies, integration with existing systems, technical specifications
- Key contacts: Environmental consulting firms, engineering companies

### Email Format
- **Length:** 300-400 words maximum
- **Tone:** Professional yet conversational, optimistic but credible
- **Personalization:** Reference recipient's organization/role in opening
- **Attribution:** Link to GROW project website, cite specific researchers
- **Signature:** Your title, Luminous Bio Solutions, contact info, LinkedIn profile

## Risk Management

### Potential Challenges
1. **Skepticism about wetlands at scale** - Address with cost data and pilot results
2. **Regulatory uncertainty** - Acknowledge pathway challenges, emphasize monitoring capability
3. **Competition from other technologies** - Position as complementary, not exclusive
4. **Indigenous consultation concerns** - Emphasize social licence pillar of GROW research
5. **Timeline expectations** - Be realistic about long-term nature of remediation

### Communication Guidelines
- **Never overstate** - Use "promising," "potential," "pilot results suggest" rather than definitive claims
- **Always attribute** - Credit GROW researchers, cite data sources
- **Stay business-focused** - You're providing business perspective, not speaking for researchers
- **Acknowledge complexity** - Don't oversimplify the science or challenges
- **Invite dialogue** - Position as learning and collaborating, not preaching
