### 2. Content Creation Workflow

**Weekly Process:**
1. **Monday:** Review content calendar for the week's theme
2. **Tuesday:** Draft post based on template structure (150-250 words)
3. **Wednesday:** Generate or select visual asset (AI video/image)
4. **Wednesday PM:** Review post and visual together, ensure cohesion
5. **Thursday AM:** Schedule or post to LinkedIn (optimal posting time: 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM MT)
6. **Thursday-Sunday:** Monitor and respond to comments within 24 hours
7. **Sunday PM:** Track engagement metrics, note what resonated

**Time Commitment:**
- Post creation: 30-45 minutes
- Visual generation/selection: 15-30 minutes
- Comment engagement: 15-30 minutes over 3-4 days
- **Total per week: ~1.5-2 hours**

### 3. AI Visual Content Generation

**Using Google Veo (Video):**
1. Access Google Labs or your AI video generation tool
2. Copy prompt from AI_Visual_Content_Prompts.md
3. Paste into generation interface
4. Generate 2-3 variations
5. Select best result for download
6. Trim to 15-30 seconds if needed
7. Add subtle caption/title card if tool allows
8. Export in MP4 format for LinkedIn compatibility

**Using Google Imagen or Similar (Images):**
1. Access image generation tool
2. Copy infographic prompt
3. Generate multiple variations
4. Select clearest, most professional result
5. Edit if needed to add: logos, specific data, text clarity
6. Export in PNG format (1200x627 or 1080x1080 for LinkedIn)
7. Run through accessibility check (color contrast, readability)

**Batch Generation Strategy:**
- Block 2-3 hours to generate Priority 1 assets before launch
- Generate Priority 2-3 assets in second session (Week 4-5)
- Generate Priority 4-5 assets in third session (Week 10-12)
- This approach minimizes context-switching and maintains visual consistency

**Tools Recommended:**
- **Video:** Google Veo, Runway Gen-3, Pika, or similar
- **Images:** Google Imagen, DALL-E 3, Midjourney, or similar
- **Editing:** CapCut (free, easy video editing), Canva (infographic refinement)

### 5. Engagement Strategy

**Responding to Comments:**

**DO:**
- Respond to all substantive comments within 24 hours
- Ask follow-up questions to deepen discussion
- Tag other experts when appropriate (e.g., "@GROW Project team...")
- Thank people for sharing perspectives
- Acknowledge pushback graciously and invite dialogue
- Use comments to identify potential collaboration opportunities

**DON'T:**
- Argue or get defensive about criticism
- Overpromise on technology capabilities
- Speak on behalf of GROW researchers (share your perspective only)
- Ignore negative comments (address respectfully)
- Leave comments unanswered for more than 48 hours

**Building Discussion:**

Use your engagement question strategically:
- Early posts: Broad questions to encourage participation
- Mid-series: Technical questions to engage experts
- Late posts: Forward-looking questions about implementation
- Always: Questions that make responders feel valued for their expertise

**Proactive Engagement:**

Spend 15-20 minutes daily:
- Comment on relevant posts from target audience members
- Share others' content about oil sands, environmental innovation, Alberta energy
- Congratulate connections on new roles, company announcements
- Engage with GROW researchers' posts (if they're on LinkedIn)
- Join relevant LinkedIn groups (Alberta energy, environmental engineering)

### 8. Risk Management & Communication Guidelines

**Potential Issues & Responses:**

**Issue: Someone claims wetlands can't work at required scale**
Response: "You raise an important scaling challenge. The GROW research specifically addresses this through [cite relevant findings]. We're not suggesting wetlands alone solve everything—integration with complementary technologies and phased implementation are part of the path forward. What scale considerations concern you most?"

**Issue: Competitor technology criticism**
Response: "There are multiple promising approaches to tailings remediation, and I'm encouraged by all innovation in this space. Wetlands offer particular advantages in [cost/energy/sustainability], but the right solution likely involves combining technologies. Are you working with alternative approaches?"

**Issue: Indigenous consultation concerns**
Response: "Absolutely critical point. Social licence and Indigenous partnership are essential—not just for regulatory approval but for ethical responsibility. The GROW project includes social science research on exactly these considerations. Luminous Bio Solutions is committed to engaging respectfully with all stakeholders. Thank you for raising this."

**Issue: Regulatory skepticism**
Response: "Fair concern. The regulatory pathway for treated water release is complex and rightly so. That's precisely why robust monitoring using validated methods like our biosensors matters—building regulatory confidence through data. What regulatory requirements do you see as most challenging?"

**Issue: Criticism of oil sands industry**
Response: "I understand the concerns about oil sands development. My focus is on addressing the environmental legacy that exists—1.4 trillion litres that need remediation regardless of perspectives on future production. These lands deserve restoration. Can we find common ground on cleaning up what's already there?"

**General Principles:**
- Stay in your lane (business perspective, not speaking for researchers)
- Acknowledge complexity and challenges
- Invite dialogue rather than debate
- Focus on solutions, not defensiveness
- Be optimistic but realistic
- Credit others' expertise generously

**Qualifying Leads:**

Watch for these engagement signals:

- Questions about implementation specifics
- Direct messages asking about Luminous Bio Solutions
- Sharing your content within their organizations

**Moving to Conversation:**

When someone shows consistent interest:
1. Send personalized connection request (if not yet connected)
2. Comment directly on their content to build rapport

4. Propose 20-minute exploratory conversation (low commitment)

**First 20-minute call:**
- 5 min: Their background and current challenges

**Key Principle: Provide value before asking for anything**

### 14. Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge: Running out of time**
Solution: Batch creation (write 4 posts in 2 hours monthly), lower posting frequency if needed (bi-weekly vs weekly), simplify visuals (stock photos vs AI generation)

**Challenge: Low initial engagement**
Solution: Proactively engage others' content first, tag relevant people/organizations, post at optimal times, use more compelling hooks, add more visual content

**Challenge: Negative or skeptical comments**
Solution: Welcome skepticism as dialogue opportunity, acknowledge valid concerns, stay solution-focused, never get defensive, model the collaborative tone you want

**Challenge: Imposter syndrome about thought leadership**
Solution: You have unique business perspective that researchers may lack, focus on translation not original research, cite experts generously, position as learning publicly not expert proclamation

**Challenge: Content feels repetitive**
Solution: Vary format (stats vs stories vs questions), change visual style, profile different researchers, explore adjacent topics (regulatory, social licence), share others' content with commentary

**Challenge: Unclear if it's working**
Solution: Track metrics consistently, quality matters more than quantity (1 meaningful connection > 100 likes), success builds slowly, patience and consistency are key, business outcomes lag engagement by months
