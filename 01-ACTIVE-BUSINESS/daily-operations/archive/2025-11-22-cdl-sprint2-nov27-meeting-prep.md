## Key Learnings

### 1. Dave Lee's "Make It Easy" Philosophy
Operators don't want to figure out if your solution makes sense. They want:
- Specific pricing with clear ROI math
- Minimal work on their side
- Pre-answered objections
- Risk mitigation (guarantees, data ownership)

### 2. Cost Recovery as Sales Strategy
Removing profit margin:
- Eliminates "vendor margin" negotiation
- Signals relationship investment
- Makes 50% grant matching even more compelling
- Positions pilot as "proving value" not "selling service"

### 3. Deliverables = Lasting Assets
Framing pilot outputs as permanent operational assets:
- Justifies budget internally ("here's what we get")
- Creates stickiness (switching = starting over)
- Sets up expansion conversation ("imagine this at all sites")
- Supports regulatory submission (baseline documentation)

### 4. Volume-Based vs. Per-Test Pricing
"More samples = higher resolution" aligns incentives:
- Operator wants maximum data
- Luminous wants maximum validation
- Removes "how few tests can we get away with" mindset
